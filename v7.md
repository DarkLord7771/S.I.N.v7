# S.I.N. v7 Evidence Ledger

**Status:** active public research laboratory; not yet a validated successor to v6.

**Evidence snapshot:** 2026-06-29.

**Working maxim:** Build from the survivors.

## Purpose

S.I.N.v6 remains the active runtime and evidence source. S.I.N.v7 now exists as an independent public research laboratory for recovering the strongest ideas from earlier S.I.N. projects, testing them honestly, and determining whether they justify a successor architecture.

v7 is not a reward for finishing v6, a larger collection of subsystems, or a new layer of lore. The repository has earned a place to conduct the experiment; a successor runtime still must be earned by demonstrating materially different value that would be dishonest or impractical to express as a bounded v6 modification.

## Evidence vocabulary

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Verified** | Reproduced by a focused test or runtime observation with inspectable output. |
| **Observed** | Present in source or artifacts, but not yet reproduced strongly enough to establish behavior. |
| **Candidate** | Worth testing; neither inherited truth nor an implementation commitment. |
| **Broken / refuted** | The current implementation fails, or the available evidence does not support the claim. |
| **Unknown** | Evidence has not yet been gathered. |

Every future claim should name its source, reproduction command, baseline, failure mode, and current label.

## Working rules

1. Task performance comes before ontology or identity language.
2. Quaternion machinery must outperform a simpler real-valued baseline under comparable data, parameters, and compute.
3. Lore may generate hypotheses, but it cannot satisfy an acceptance criterion.
4. Distinguish a mathematical primitive from its integration. Source presence is not runtime proof.
5. Keep declared anchors, fitted estimates, live trajectories, and persistent learning separate.
6. Do not call random motion cognition, repeated motion memory, a basin a SoulChain, or artifact accumulation learning.
7. Start with a deterministic research harness and CLI evidence. UI, rigging, personality, and presentation come later.
8. Candidate learning remains shadow-only until it improves held-out performance, survives restart, repeats, and can be rolled back.
9. Continue v6 observation without allowing v7 work to rewrite its evidence or authority boundaries.

## Provisional thesis

The strongest inherited idea is not a wholly quaternion replacement for an LLM or visual model. It is a heterogeneous bilateral system joined by a quaternion-valued **CorpusCallosum**:

```text
Language/task -> left encoder -> quaternion projector --\
                                                        +-> Q-callosum -> memory -> task head
Visual/task   -> right AVR    -> quaternion projector --/
                              ^
                      calibrated Thalamus router
```

- **Left stream:** language, rules, sequences, causal hypotheses, and critique.
- **Right stream:** abstract visual reasoning, spatial transformations, visual counterexamples, and generative exploration.
- **Thalamus:** calibrated routing from uncertainty, novelty, conflict, provenance, and cost.
- **CorpusCallosum:** an explicit fusion seam whose quaternion operations compete against simpler fusion baselines.
- **Memory:** trajectories and outcomes, not isolated points presented as identity.

This is a hypothesis, not the v7 architecture.

## Quaternion representation contract

Before selecting layers, v7 must decide what a quaternion means in each location.

### Orientation-bearing state

Use a unit quaternion and separate magnitude/confidence:

```text
h = rho * q_hat
q_hat in S^3
rho >= 0
```

- Preserve antipodal equivalence `q_hat ~ -q_hat` when the state represents orientation.
- Normalize intentionally; do not erase useful amplitude by normalizing every activation blindly.
- Use log/exp maps and geodesic distance for manifold operations.

### General hypercomplex features

If four channels are simply a quaternion-valued feature, do not automatically interpret them as an orientation or attach fixed emotional meanings to `w/x/y/z`.

- Define quaternion linear/convolution/recurrent layers through the Hamilton product and explicit weight sharing.
- Compare against a real-valued layer with matched parameter and compute budgets.
- Probe learned semantics after training rather than declaring axis meanings beforehand.

### Required property tests

- normalization and zero-norm behavior;
- Hamilton-product identities and noncommutativity;
- `exp(log(q))` round-trip within a stated domain;
- SLERP endpoints, shortest path, and near-collinear stability;
- `q` / `-q` equivalence where applicable;
- batched and scalar parity;
- gradient finiteness and deterministic seeded runs;
- checkpoint/restart equivalence.

## Candidate callosal mechanisms

Quaternion fusion is only one competitor:

1. Concatenation plus MLP.
2. Learned scalar/vector gating.
3. Ordinary cross-attention.
4. Tangent-space weighted averaging.
5. SLERP with learned interpolation.
6. Weighted Karcher fusion.
7. Hamilton-product mixing.
8. Geodesic cross-attention, for example `softmax(-d(q, k)^2 / tau)` with learned temperature.

The callosum survives only if its contribution appears in ablation results.

## First decisive experiment

### Research question

Can a bilateral language-plus-AVR system solve held-out abstract visual reasoning tasks better than either stream alone and better than simpler fusion under a comparable budget?

### Initial conditions

- Objective tasks with exact or programmatically verifiable answers, such as ARC-style grid transformations or controlled spatial game-state problems.
- A frozen or tightly bounded left language/rule encoder.
- A frozen or tightly bounded right visual/spatial encoder.
- Small learned projectors into the shared representation.
- One task head and one reproducible training/evaluation harness.

### Required comparisons

| Condition | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Left only | Establish language/rule baseline. |
| Right only | Establish visual/spatial baseline. |
| Simple concatenation | Test whether ordinary fusion is sufficient. |
| Learned gate or cross-attention | Establish a strong conventional fusion baseline. |
| Quaternion callosum variants | Test the actual v7 hypothesis. |

### Metrics

- held-out and out-of-distribution task accuracy;
- calibration and abstention quality;
- parameter count, training compute, latency, and memory;
- sensitivity to rotations/reflections when the task warrants it;
- restart/checkpoint reproducibility;
- failure taxonomy and hemisphere-disagreement behavior.

### Graduation rule

The bilateral quaternion approach earns further architecture only after repeated runs show a meaningful advantage over the individual streams and the strongest simple-fusion baseline. One beautiful trace is not sufficient.

## Learning and consolidation proof

A later experiment may test whether one bounded experience creates a predicted, beneficial, persistent change.

The update must:

1. be proposed in shadow mode;
2. name the experience and affected parameters or memory entries;
3. improve a preregistered held-out measure;
4. survive checkpoint/restart;
5. repeat across independent cycles;
6. preserve calibration and bounded failure behavior;
7. support explicit rollback.

Dreaming or replay counts as learning only when this full chain is demonstrated.

## Explicitly left behind

- hardcoded emotions or identity claims attached to quaternion axes;
- random drift presented as cognition;
- reversed input through one shared matrix presented as specialized hemispheres;
- manual symbolic thresholds presented as discovered laws;

## Boundary: modify v6 or graduate work into v7?

### Modify v6 if

- the desired change is bounded to its existing runtime, continuity, reporting, or operator workflow;
- a conventional fusion method is sufficient;
- no new bilateral learning contract is required;
- the evidence does not justify a different architectural center.

### Graduate work into a v7 runtime if

- heterogeneous LLM/AVR reasoning becomes the central runtime contract;
- the callosum/router/memory experiment demonstrates repeatable value;
- persistent learning requires a smaller, explicitly experimental architecture;
- carrying v6's observer and presentation stack would obscure or bias the experiment.

Until those conditions are met, S.I.N.v7 is an independent notebook and laboratory—not a validated successor product.
