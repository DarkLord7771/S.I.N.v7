# Sources and inspiration

External material can motivate a hypothesis, but it is not implementation
evidence for S.I.N.v7. This file records provenance separately from the
project's evidence ledger.

## Rotational neural dynamics after distraction

The repository initially used `inspiration.png`, a temporary screenshot of:

- Picower Institute at MIT, “MIT scientists discover how the brain spins back
  into focus,” published by ScienceDaily on November 4, 2025:
  <https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2025/11/251104013002.htm>
- Underlying paper: Tamal Batabyal, Scott L. Brincat, Jacob A. Donoghue,
  Mikael Lundqvist, Meredith K. Mahnke, and Earl K. Miller,
  “State-Space Trajectories and Traveling Waves Following Distraction,”
  *Journal of Cognitive Neuroscience* (2025), DOI:
  <https://doi.org/10.1162/JOCN.a.2410>

The article reports rotational state-space trajectories and traveling cortical
waves during recovery from distraction. S.I.N.v7 treats that result as
biological and mathematical inspiration only. It does not establish that a
quaternion neural architecture is necessary, equivalent, or beneficial.

The screenshot has been removed while an original project image is prepared.
